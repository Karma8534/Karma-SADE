But here’s the thing: you don’t need 22K stars to get in. Let me break down
exactly what this program is, who qualifies, and how to put together a solid
application.
What Is the Claude for Open Source Program?
Anthropic just launched this program to give back to open-source
maintainers and contributors. The deal is simple: if you qualify, you get 6
months of Claude Max 20x, that’s the $200/month tier, at zero cost. No strings
attached beyond the standard Terms of Service.
They’re accepting up to 10,000 recipients on a rolling basis. Applications are
open until June 30, 2026, or until they hit the cap. So there’s urgency here,
but it’s not a sprint.

That repo has 5,000+ GitHub stars OR 1M+ monthly npm downloads

You have merge access (write or admin permissions)
You’ve been actively contributing in the last 3 months (commits, PR

If you hit these numbers, the application is pretty much filling out a form

Track 2: Ecosystem Impact Track
This is the one most people miss. Straight from the program page: “If you
maintain something the ecosystem quietly depends on, apply anyway and
tell us about it.”
Maybe you maintain a foundational library that half the JavaScript
ecosystem depends on but only has 2K stars. Maybe you built a critical piece
of infrastructure tooling that doesn’t get the GitHub star treatment because
it’s not flashy. Anthropic explicitly invites these applications.
For this track, you write a short explanation (500 words max) about what
your project does and why it matters to the ecosystem. Think: number of
downstream dependents, breadth of usage, how critical the project is to the
things built on top of it.
General Requirements
Regardless of which track:
You must be a real person (not a company)
18+ years old

Legal resident of a country where Claude is available
Have a GitHub account in good standing
Not an Anthropic employee or family member of one
How to Apply
1. Go to claude.com/contact-sales/claude-for-oss
2. Fill in your GitHub username or profile URL
3. Provide your email address (this is where they’ll send the activation link
if approved)
4. If you’re applying under the Ecosystem Impact Track, write your brief
about why your project matters
5. Submit and wait
Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis. There’s no set timeline for when
you’ll hear back.
If approved, you’ll get an email with a unique activation link. You must
activate before June 30, 2026 or the subscription is forfeited.
Why This Matters
I’ve been building open-source tools for years. CodeGPT, and now Claude
Code Templates. The open-source ecosystem runs on the unpaid labor of
maintainers who rarely get recognized for the infrastructure they hold
together.

Programs like this won’t solve the sustainability problem in open source.
That requires a much deeper conversation about funding, governance, and
corporate responsibility. But giving maintainers direct access to the most
powerful AI tools available right now?
That’s a meaningful step. It helps with the day-to-day grind, code reviews,
refactoring, documentation, debugging, the stuff that burns people out.
Anthropic gets something out of this too. Of course they do. Building
adoption among influential developers is smart business. But that doesn’t
diminish the value for the developers who get in.
If you maintain open-source software and Claude Code is part of your
workflow (or could be), this is worth 10 minutes of your time.
