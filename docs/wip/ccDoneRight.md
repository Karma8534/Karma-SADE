I’ve spent the last few months watching people use
Claude. Colleagues, clients, strangers on LinkedIn.
And honestly? Most of them are doing it wrong.
Not because they’re bad at prompting. But because
they never set Claude up properly in the first place.


---
*Page 2*


Think about it like this — you wouldn’t open a code
editor, skip all configuration, and then wonder
why IntelliSense isn’t working. The same logic
applies here.
Step 1: Download Claude and Open
Cowork
Go to Claude.com/download and install the
desktop app.


---
*Page 3*


Once it’s open, look at the tabs at the top. You’ll see
one called Cowork. Click it.
You’ll need a Pro plan ($20/month) for this. I know
that sounds like an upsell, but the difference
between Chat and Cowork is significant. Cowork
lets Claude actually work with your files, not just
talk about them.
Step 2: Pick the Right Model (This Matters
More Than You Think)
Click the model dropdown. Select Opus 4.6. Then
turn on Extended Thinking.
Don’t switch these mid-project. Wrong model =
inconsistent output. It sounds minor until you’ve
rerun the same prompt six times and can’t figure
out why the output keeps changing.
Opus 4.6 with Extended Thinking is slower, but it
reasons through problems instead of just pattern-


---
*Page 4*


matching to an answer. For anything complex, that
gap is huge.
Step 3: Build Your Context Folder
This is where most people stop reading. Don’t.
Create one master folder on your computer. Inside
it, make four subfolders:
/MyClaudeSetup
├── ABOUT_ME/
├── PROJECTS/
├── TEMPLATES/
└── OUTPUTS/
Each one has a purpose:
ABOUT_ME:- Who you are and how you write
PROJECTS:- One subfolder per active project
TEMPLATES:- Your best past work, saved as
structure


---
*Page 5*


OUTPUTS:- Where Claude saves finished files
Upload this folder to Cowork. That’s how Claude
“reads” you before it does anything.
Without this, you’re prompting a stranger. With it,
you’re briefing someone who already knows your
context.
Step 4: Write Three Context Files Inside
ABOUT_ME
These three .md files replace prompting. This is
the actual setup.
/ABOUT_ME
├── about-me.md
├── my-voice.md
└── my-rules.md
Here’s what goes in each:

Step 5: Set Global Instructions (Once,
Then Forget It)
Go to Settings → Cowork → Edit Global
Instructions.
Paste this:
I'm [Name], [Role]. Read my files before every task.
Ask clarifying questions before executing.


---
*Page 8*


Show a plan before acting.
Never delete without my approval.
Replace [Name] and [Role] with your actual info.
That’s it. You set this once. It runs automatically at
the start of every Cowork session.
After this, your prompts can be 10 words. Claude
already has the context it needs.
Before Every Session, Check Three Things
1. Am I in Cowork (not Chat)?
2. Is Opus 4.6 + Extended Thinking on?
3. Did I point it to my folder with context files?
Set those first. Then prompt.
Why This Actually Works


---
*Page 9*


Here’s the thing about prompting — you’re not just
giving instructions. You’re reducing Claude’s
uncertainty.
When Claude doesn’t know who you are, what
you’ve done before, or how you want things
delivered, it fills in the gaps with generic
assumptions. Generic assumptions = generic
output.
The folder system, the .md files, the global
instructions — all of it is just structured context.
You're not "hacking" anything. You're just being a
better communicator.
It’s the same reason a good junior hire
outperforms a talented contractor in week two.
The hire has context. The contractor is still
guessing.
One More Thing


---
*Page 10*


None of this is complicated. It’s 30 minutes of
setup. But almost nobody does it, because most
guides skip straight to prompts and templates.
Who actually gets good output from Claude? They
did the boring part first.
Now you can too.
