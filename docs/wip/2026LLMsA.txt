To run a Qwen 3.5-27B (Dense) model on your system, you have to utilize a hybrid approach called GGUF Offloading.
Since your RTX 3070 has 8GB of VRAM and the 27B model (even at 4-bit compression) requires ~17GB, the model will not fit entirely on your graphics card. Instead, your software (like Ollama or LM Studio) will split the model into layers and distribute them across your hardware.
1. The VRAM vs. System RAM Split
LLMs consist of multiple "layers" (think of them as building blocks). For Qwen 3.5-27B, there are typically 64 layers.
 * GPU (VRAM): Your 8GB will hold roughly 20–25 layers. These layers will process at lightning speed.
 * System RAM: Your massive 64GB of RAM will hold the remaining ~40 layers.
 * The Bottleneck: The overall speed will be limited by how fast data can travel from your system RAM to your CPU. Because you have 64GB of RAM, the model will "fit" easily, but you won't get the 50+ tokens/second seen on high-end GPUs. Expect roughly 3–7 tokens per second (typical reading speed).
2. Implementation Strategy
To get this running effectively for your Karma SADE project, use these specific settings to maximize your 8GB VRAM:
Use GGUF with "K-Quants"
Do not use the standard 4-bit (Q4_0). Instead, look for Q4_K_M or IQ4_XS. These "Importance Quants" keep the most critical parts of the brain (the attention layers) at higher precision while compressing the rest.
Quantize the KV Cache
The "memory" of your conversation (KV Cache) also takes up space. If you are doing long-context agentic work, your VRAM will fill up fast.
 * In your settings, enable 4-bit or 8-bit KV Cache quantization.
 * This can save you 2-4GB of VRAM, allowing you to fit more model layers onto the GPU.
3. Why this works for your hardware
Your Intel Core Ultra 9 185H and 64GB of RAM are actually the "secret weapons" here.
 * Bandwidth: While your GPU is faster, the Core Ultra 9 has high memory bandwidth for a laptop, which makes CPU-inference less painful than on older chips.
 * Stability: Because you aren't "red-lining" your VRAM (by offloading the rest to your 64GB), you avoid the system crashes or "Out of Memory" (OOM) errors that plague 8GB users with only 16GB of system RAM.
